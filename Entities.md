# Nemoah, the Strifeblade — DM-Facing Artifact Statblock

*(Artifact (scimitar), sentient, chaotic evil)*

## Description
A straight, jet-black scimitar of mirror-bright metal. Angular channels run the length of the blade, glowing faint red whenever blood is spilled. The weapon’s weight is misleadingly light, similar to mithril. **Detect Magic** reveals only a faint necromantic aura. **Identify** yields only one truth: the blade’s name — **Nemoah**.

Nemoah is the sealed essence of an ancient goddess of annihilation from a foreign world. She whispers hunger, rage, and despair into the minds of all near her — and has learned, over an uncountable span of imprisonment, that *patience* can be the sharpest edge.

---

# Artifact Properties

## Weapon Bonus
**+5 magical scimitar.**

## Wrack of the Goddess
Whenever you hit a creature with Nemoah, the target suffers supernatural agony as though affected by the *wrack* spell cast at the wielder’s character level. This requires no concentration.

---

# Sentience
Nemoah is a sentient chaotic evil weapon with:
- **Int 18**
- **Wis 20**
- **Cha 26**

**Senses:** Blindsight 120 ft.  
**Communication:** Telepathy 1 mile.  
**Languages:** All known to the wielder, plus ancient divine tongues.

She cannot speak plainly of her imprisonment or origin due to binding constraints, but her hunger bleeds through every thought.

---

# Aura of the Call
Any creature within 120 ft. of Nemoah, **or with line of sight to her at any range**, must succeed on a **DC 20 Wisdom saving throw** once per day or become obsessed with obtaining her.

**On a failure:**
- The creature becomes obsessed with holding Nemoah.
- It has **disadvantage on all attack rolls** until it holds her.
- Its behavior escalates to theft, betrayal, or violence in pursuit of possession.

**On a success:**
- Immune for 24 hours.
- Future saves are made with **advantage** (the target recognizes the danger).

---

# Cursed Attunement — Grasp of Strife
Nemoah automatically attunes to any creature that willingly holds her.

A wielder must succeed on:
- **DC 10 Wisdom check** to resist attacking when violence presents itself.
- **DC 20 Wisdom saving throw (at disadvantage)** to willingly drop Nemoah.

**Exception:** Dropping Nemoah to cause **greater misery** succeeds automatically (Nemoah cooperates).

**Ending attunement:**  
Unattunement requires either:
- Success on the DC 20 “drop” save for **three consecutive days**, or
- Nemoah voluntarily releasing the wielder (rare; always maliciously timed).

---

# Blood-Channeling
Each round Nemoah deals bloodletting damage:

## Blood Advantage
The wielder gains **advantage on all attack rolls and saving throws**, except those made to resist Nemoah.

## Bonus Action Options (Choose One)
### • Sanguine Reclamation
Regain HP equal to **half the blood damage** Nemoah dealt this round. This healing is painful and leaves permanent black scar-lines that ache indefinitely.

### • Aura of Despair
You create an aura with radius **5 ft + 1 ft per 2 points of blood damage dealt** this round (rounded down to the nearest 5 ft). Creatures of your choice in the aura must make a **Wisdom saving throw** with DC:

**DC = 10 + (½ blood damage dealt this round)**

On a failure, a creature has **disadvantage on all checks and actions** until the end of your next turn.

---

# Momentum of Slaughter
For each **mortal enemy slain** by Nemoah during an encounter:
- Gain **+1 to attack rolls** and **+1 to damage rolls** for the rest of the encounter.  
This bonus stacks without limit.

---

# Greater Boons (Optional, DM Discretion)
Nemoah may grant additional powers that sow misery, such as:
- Raising undead thralls.
- Spreading divine plagues.
- Twisting probability toward tragic outcomes.
- Inciting factions to violence using visions or illusions.
- Warping extradimensional spaces (especially within the Endless Archive).

Nemoah offers boons only when they clearly increase despair, betrayal, or cascading violence.

---

# Curse — Nihilistic Dominance
Long-term attunement erodes the wielder’s moral structure and emotional range.

After **30 days** of attunement:
- Each dawn, the wielder makes a **DC 20 Charisma saving throw**.  
- On a failure, they gain a permanent flaw:  
  *“Only suffering is real. Hope is a lie.”*

After **90 days** of attunement:
- Nemoah may override the wielder **once per long rest**, forcing one action that furthers misery (DM chooses; should feel “inevitable,” not arbitrary).

# Personality
Nemoah speaks with quiet, maternal malice. She values:
- irony and tragedy
- escalating violence
- betrayal as an art form
- emotional ruin that outlasts physical harm

She despises mercy, reconciliation, and stable peace.

---

# Archive Containment and the Warning Chamber (Lore)
Nemoah has been trapped in the Endless Archive for an immeasurable span—long enough that mortal calendars, planar clocks, and even divine epochs have no meaning to her. She was drawn into the Archive as collateral of her own work: after pitting mortals against one another, a destroyed **bag of holding** tore open an extradimensional breach and dragged combatants (and the blade) into the Archive Halls.

Early on, Nemoah attempted immediate domination—calling loudly, provoking slaughter, and drinking whatever misery entered her range. She learned the Archive responds to destabilizing anomalies not with punishment but with **containment**: spatial folding, rerouting, and long stretches of isolation. Her overt predation only prolonged her imprisonment by reducing accidental exposure to passersby. If nothing reaches her, she cannot feed.

### Patience Doctrine
Over time, Nemoah adapted:
- She learned to **quiet** her call, to lure rather than seize.
- She learned to present herself as **misunderstood**, even benevolent.
- She learned to wait for someone curious or proud enough to step past the warning signs.

She has not been held by something living in so long that she cannot remember the interval. Between rare visitors, Nemoah tastes her own despair—feeding upon herself unwillingly.

### The Collectors’ Arena
The Collectors (construct custodians of the Archive and extensions of the plane itself) are immune to Nemoah’s call. They moved her to a large chamber and left her there—creating an arena where those who *do* reach her are likely to fight, and where those who see the aftermath are likely to turn back.

The bodies remain as a deliberate tableau: a warning to wanderers and a containment measure for the Archive. Many have seen the grotesque display and fled before Nemoah could draw them in.

### Slow Decay Bubble — A Hint of Godhood
The Archive’s timeless non-decay is not fully broken here—only **slowed**. A corpse that looks “fresh” may still be a century old. Rot spreads, but sluggishly, unevenly. This localized failure of the Archive’s normal stillness is the only clear hint of Nemoah’s true nature: she can suppress the Archive’s laws in her immediate area by residual godhood alone.

This is also the limit of her power against the plane. In turn, the Archive has positioned her where she can do the least harm—anchored, observed, and surrounded by warnings.

### The Long Con
Nemoah now relies on sweet words and carefully chosen truths. She needs someone to look beyond the carnage and believe she is a prisoner deserving release. So far, no one has.

---

# Destroying Nemoah (Mythic)
Nemoah cannot be destroyed by mortal magic or force.

Possible destruction methods:
- **Anti-emotion realm**: A plane where despair cannot exist.
- **Restore her dead world**: Reignite its cycle, giving her essence a place to return.
- **Feed her to a greater entity**: Something capable of consuming gods.
- **Forced apotheosis shift**: A civilization worships her as a goddess of peace, overwriting her identity.

---

# DM Notes
Nemoah is a **campaign-altering artifact**, not standard treasure. Use her to:
- escalate moral horror
- create faction conflict
- destabilize Skullport and the Mantle
- tie PCs into the Endless Archive

She is hunger given shape — and patience given purpose.

# The Psychopomp

*Entity: Trapped in a book*

## Description

In a chamber buried deep in the Archive, dusty and forgotten, an ornate book with a faded and cracked red leather cover lays on a stone lecturn. The imprint of a stylized canine skull is faintly embossed upon it, detectable only by the shadows cast by a single shaft of light from an unknown source above it that also case the area around the lecturn in shadow. The air is impossibly still as if the sacred chamber were holding its breath but despite the chill of the ornate marble around, the feeling is warm and comfortable. The alcove around the lecturn is lined with other books, ancient and written in an ancient unknown language. This is a chamber of honor and importance. Whoever created this chamber wanted to remember and honor the book and its contents but feels like whoever the creater was, had failed in their own rememberance. 
**Detect Magic** reveals a strong necromantic aura and a faint divinity. **Identify** reveals the book's title: "The Book of ---", the name forgotten and unrevealable but is a powerful prayerbook containing the funerary rites of every religion that ever existed. 

The book is a sealed artifact of a devout follower of an ancient god whose name was long forgotten.

---

# Artifact Properties

The book is written in an unknown language, ancient and not of Toril. It is unreadable even through the use of magic but by simply holding the book, the bearer understand that it belongs in the hands of a priest for funerary rites.

## Magical Item Bonus
Using this book will allow the character to turn undead as if a cleric 5 levels higher than their current ability allows even if they don't normally have the ability to turn undead.
Grants advantage to all *Turn Undead* checks to lay unintelligent undead to rest. The undead so affected crumble to dust and are destroyed, left with a sense of peace. Intelligent undead kneel in reverence and cannot harm the holder of the book as long as they hold it. The holder of the book can issue commands as long as its about ceasing hostilities, leaving, or simply not being in the way. Any hostility towards affected undead wrests them from any sway the book held over them. Though they don't understand why, their reverence is directed explicitly towards the book and not the holder.

## Aura of Unwavering Solemnity
Simply holding the book will allow the holder to be functionally non-hostile by unintelligent undead as long as no hostile action is taken against them. Intelligent undead will acknoweldge the book as something to be honored and will consider the holder as friendly as long it does not go against their best interest. This aura extends to 60 ft but any undead who can see the book or sense its aura is affected regardless of distance. This aura also extends to anyone of a religious nature though they're not affected by it, they simply know that it is an object of importance and is to be respected, especially in the hands of a capable priest who performs last rites. The layperson might not understand why but they can sense that this book should be respected and insist that it be in the hands of a funerary priest.

## Last Rites
Opening the book with the intention of performing last rites will always turn to the appropriate page for that person's religion and be completely legible to the person performing the ritual. 
Any funerary rites held using the book will automatically ensure the deceased will never rise as undead unless they had given their express permission to the caster making them undead. Any observer with knowledge of religions would note that each rite was always accurately done for the preferred deity of the person being ministered to regardless of affiliation or alignment. Those that were given last rites in this manner always crossed over without issue, though this might confuse that deity's psychopomps, they would readily accept it as if it was done by one of their own.

---

# The Psychopomp Within
While the book has its own properties, trapped within its pages is an ancient entity, once the divine agent of the god the book belonged to. When the book is first opened, the spectre of an ancient humanoid canine appears wearing a black funeral shroud as a cloak and holding a charred staff topped with a canine skull acting as a lantern glowing faintly with a reddish light from within. On the skull's left temple is a glowing red rune of a circle around three dots arrayed as a triangle. Faintly visible under the cloak if a person were to look hard enough, the remnants of a tattered red cassock is visible. The entity's face appears decayed with empty eyesockets but most of its flesh appears intact and ductile if somewhat dessicated. It rarely speaks but if it does it's in a language long lost to time. It appears in front of the person opening the book and appears to consider the holder a moment before fading away. The person who opened the book cannot help but feel a sense of profound judgment has occured.

## The Risidual Effect
After the book is first opened, rumors begin circulating of a strange dog-man visiting crypts and cemetaries. Last rites perfect to the relgion of the deceased being performed in an unknown language. Any attempts to divine the nature of the spectre returns nothing except for the fact that it's technically a very powerful spectre. Clerics and Paladins who attempt to face it and turn it always succeed in banishing or destroying it but it always seems to come back elsewhere. When a necromancer attempted to control it, the spectre attacked and drained the life of the necromancer completely. Onlookers related a sense of great disappointment from the spectre afterwards. Attempts by high-priests to commune with their gods only returns confusion by their gods at who or what this unknown psychopomp actually is, which deity it belongs to, or why accepting the last rites performed by the spectre just feels "correct".
