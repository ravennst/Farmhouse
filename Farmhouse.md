Journal of Francois DuPlessis.

I found the odd door standing in the middle of the woods, just standing there, about 20 years ago. It wasn't a style common to the valley so I have no idea where it came from. I tried to knock it over but wouldnt budge. It had no frame and dispite how it stood. Picking it up made it seem impossibly light but when I looked closer it seemed to have been carved from a single piece of wood. The doorknob was also of a style and metal I had never seen before but it turned smoothly and the latch would go in and out without issue. 
I prayed to god that this wasn't some sort of evil thing. I should have trusted my instincts.

Despite the odd nature of it, I found it to be a lovely door and took it home to figure out what I could do with it. Carrying it was also odd, it was light and resisted being flipped over. Why I ignored this, I do not know.

The door stood in the barn for the longest time, hidden behind the hay, forgotten for two years until I needed a new front door on the house.
Why I remembered the devil's door then, only the good lord knows. I hope God can forgive me for my sins.

The door fit impossibly perfect in the door frame of the house, like it had been specifically made for it. The moment I placed it, it was already attached to the frame. Hinges that weren't there manifested while I checked the fit. The plate for the latch appeared when I wasn't looking. The door opened smoothly on well-oiled hinges and latched cleanly without rubbing. It felt like this door had always been there. This is when I first realized that observation kept its evil from manifesting. I would only realize too late how important observation would be when it came to the devil's door.

I believe the door has a way to alter how you feel about. To not question it how impossible its existence is. Now it's too late for me. I have no idea where the house is. I cannot even look out the windows anymore. 

Everything was fine for another year after I installed it. My wife never even questioned it, even praising me for making a fantastic door. I had hoped that if I never said anything to her about it, it would leave her alone. I guess it did, as when the door stopped opening to the valley, her and the baby weren't in the house. I knew then I had made a grave mistake, condemmed to the firey lake of damnation for the end of time. I tried to remove the door but nothing worked. The windows wouldn't shatter. I could open the door but it opened up to the middle of a field. The trees were wrong, unrecognizable from anything that grew in the valley. I looked back at the house and only saw the door with the sun shining overhead. I could see the hallway to the kitchen through it but only the forest around the frame, standing tall on its own in the middle of the woods like when I first came acrossed the cursed thing.

I was afraid. Afraid that I was in a place that God might not find me. Afraid that I had died an unforgiven sinner. 

I ran into the kitchen and grabbed the bottle of holy water. I had slammed the door shut and splashed it with the water, praying to the Lord to forgive and save me. 
It only made the door wet.

I spent a long time praying for forgivness, until my knees were sore and fell to the floor, exhausted.

I went to the storm cellar to get my axe from the stairs that led to the storm cellar door. Going outside the front door was pointless, the house had no outside here.
Whatever had happened to the house, the storm celler was there but empty. I had no idea what happned to the stores we kept down here. The axe was there on the stairs by the cellar doors leading outside. I had heard movement outside the doors and saw a shadow flicker through the gap between the two door panels. I put out the lantern and put it down whlie slowly grabbing the axe, staring through the crack of the door as I moved closer. 

Whatever was out there hadn't noticed me yet and while I couldn't see it, I could hear it towards the back of the house. I moved up to the doors and peeked through the crack. It was dark but I could see, like the twilight just before the sun goes down. I could see grass just beyond but where I had expected the barn to be, I saw nothing. I tried to look past all that, to see the mountain and the trees, but I saw nothing. I didn't see a horizon or a sky. Where ever the house was, it definitely wasn't in the valley I knew anymore. What I did see in the grey twiight was a wall, a wall of fancy wood panels with an impossibly high ceiling. I felt like I was in a doll house in a giant child's room. I prayed often during this time. I should have been paying attention to the creature outside.

It must have gone around the house entirely, coming now from the front and back to the storm cellar doors. I had been so enthralled by the strange wall in front of me that I hadn't noticed it until it sniffed at the crack again, this time having caught my scent. I jumped back in fright, uttering blasphemes that shame me to this day. I had originally assumed it was some sort of dog or coyote but it was impossibly large. I stood up with axe in hand, praying for forgiveness and protection. It stopped snuffling and instead gave a satisfied growl. I prayed harder as I heard the latch move and a hand slowly open the storm door. A devil beast stood in the stairs, a man of a rat with teeth the size of mortise chisles and claws the size of a bear's. 
It lunged and I felt the axe bury itself into its skull. The great Lord God saw to it to guide my hand as the creature writhed at my feet in its death throes. 

I saw fit to remove its demonic head from its shoulders. It had been hunched over before but was as tall as a man when laid straight. The devil did have the face of a rodent, its tail was easily five feet long on its own. This was the first of the beasts I would find within the halls of hell I found myself in. I thank the Lord that it was the only one to find me that first month. I had barred the storm doors from within the cellar so I set flame to the devil in the forest outside the front doors and then buried the ashes with a crucifix carved from the local trees, one in the grave and one above it.

I used this time to gather firewood and forage for food and water. I had found many fruits and plants unknown to me but they sustained me. I would soon discover another property of the house, nothing brought in ever decayed. I had no idea what this meant, but at least the food would keep. The forest had low hills and I even found a lake. Just like the other things I found in the forest, I didn't recognize the fish but they tasted just fine when roasted. I had begun to think that perhaps I should build a new home in this strange land, burn down the house with the devil's door and be rid of it. The only reason I had not yet was the hope that I would somehow open that door back to the valley I knew as home and see my wife and infant son. I pray constantly for them to be safe, I doubt now that I will ever see them again.

The hope I could be rid of the devil's door was shattered when a creature resembling a hairless moose with a bull's horns charged through the trees to the edge of the lake followed by a scaled bear the size of the house I was building. The scaled bear chased the hairless thing towards me, eventually crashing though the structure like it was made of twigs. I had never run so fast as I did then back to the door in the woods. At some point the scaled bear stopped chasing the hairless moose-thing and set its sight on me. I made it into the old house of mine just in time and slammed the door, praying to be anywhere but where I was. As soon as the door latched closed, it was suddenly silent. This nearly made me more afraid than the creature outside made me. I waited for so long but heard nothing. I tried to peek out but the door wouldn't open, the knob wouldn't even turn.

I spent a week locked in this house of perdition before I decided to venture out the only other door I could access. The back door was locked shut this entire time. Even the axe wouldn't open either door. That left the storm door in the cellar.

So far I have only made one trip into hell's halls outside the storm doors. I found this book, largely blank so that I decided to start writing about what has happened to me. If I never make it back home, I hope if anyone finds this, they do not commit the same sins I have. May God have mercy on us all.
